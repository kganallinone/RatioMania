Example for How to Convert a Terminating Decimal to a Fraction

Convert the terminating decimal 0.2 to a fraction in simplest form.
Step 1: First, we determine the place value of the last digit of the decimal, 2. The last digit is in the tenths place.
Step 2: Since the last digit of the decimal is in the tenths place, we write the fraction with a denominator of 10. When we write the decimal as a fraction, we no longer need the decimal point. Therefore, 0.2= 2/10
Step 3: Finally, we must simplify the fraction from the previous step until it is its simplest form.
2/10=(2÷2)/(10÷2)=1/5 (Divide the numerator and denominator by 2)

Non-terminating


There is an easy trick to convert a repeating decimal to the fraction form. Write the repeating digits as the numerator over the same number of 9s.
Repeating Decimal of the Form 0.(wxyz) ̅…
This is applicable only for repeating decimals having only repeating digits after the decimal point.
We can write a formula to convert such decimals to fractions as
0.(wxyz) ̅= (Repeated digits or digits)/(Number of 9^' s equal to the number of repeated digits)

Example 1: 0.(2222) ̅…= 2/9
There is only 1 repeating digit, 2. So, the denominator will be 9.

Example 2: 0.(3232) ̅…= 32/99
There are 2 repeating digits, 3 and 2. So, the numerator will be 32, and two 9’s or 99 will come in the denominator.

Example 3: 0.(9999) ̅…= 9/9=1

Step 1: Determine how many decimal places are in the given number.
Step 2: Remove the decimal point from the number and divide it by a power of ten equal to the number of decimal places. So, if there are two decimal places, we must divide the number by 102, which is 100. To put it another way, the number of zeros should be equal to the number of decimal places.
Step 3: Simplify the fraction and, if necessary, convert it to a mixed number if it is an improper fraction.

A non-terminating but recurring decimal number can be converted to its rational number equivalent as
Step 1: Assume the repeating decimal to be equal to some variable x.
Step 2: Write the number without using a bar and equal to x.
Step 3: Determine the number of digits having a bar on their heads or the number of digits before the bar for mixed recurring decimal.
Step 4: If the repeating number is the same digit after the decimal such as 0.333333… then multiply by 10, if repetition of the digits is in pairs of two numbers such as 0.252525… then multiply by 100 and so on
Step 5: Subtract the equation formed by step 2 and step 4.
Step 6: Find the value of x in the simplest form.

Example 1: Convert 1.343434… or =1.(34) ̅ to a rational number.
Step 1: Let x=1.343434…
Since 2 digit is repeated multiply both sides by 100
100 x x = 100 x 1.343434…
Step 2: 100x = 134.343434…
Subtract whole number by 1.
99x = 133 (divide both sides by 99)
X= 133/99
Therefore, 1.343434… or 1.(34) ̅ = 133/99 = 1 34/99

Example 2: Convert 6.27454545… or =6.27(45) ̅ to a rational number.
Step 1: Let x=6.27454545…
Since 2 numbers 2 and 7 are not repeated, therefore, multiply both sides by 100.
